# Islamic Knowledge Base - Al-Kitab Al-Mubeen

## Five Pillars of Islam (أركان الإسلام الخمسة)

### 1. Shahada (الشهادة) - Declaration of Faith
The Shahada is the Islamic declaration of faith and the first pillar of Islam. It states:
**"Lā ilāha illā Allāhu, Muḥammadur rasūlu Allāhi"**
*"There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger."*

**Significance:**
- Foundation of Islamic belief
- Entry point into Islam
- Recited daily in prayers
- Must be said with sincere belief and understanding

### 2. Salah (الصلاة) - Prayer
The five daily prayers are the second pillar of Islam:

**The Five Daily Prayers:**
1. **Fajr** - Dawn prayer (2 rak'ahs)
2. **Dhuhr** - Midday prayer (4 rak'ahs)
3. **Asr** - Afternoon prayer (4 rak'ahs)
4. **Maghrib** - Sunset prayer (3 rak'ahs)
5. **Isha** - Night prayer (4 rak'ahs)

**Prayer Requirements:**
- Ritual purity (Wudu)
- Facing the Qibla (direction of Kaaba)
- Appropriate dress
- Intention (Niyyah)

### 3. Zakat (الزكاة) - Obligatory Charity
Zakat is the third pillar and a form of almsgiving treated as a religious obligation.

**Key Principles:**
- 2.5% of wealth above nisab threshold
- Given to eight categories of recipients mentioned in Quran
- Purifies wealth and soul
- Creates social solidarity

**Zakat Recipients (Quran 9:60):**
1. The poor (Al-Fuqara)
2. The needy (Al-Masakin)
3. Zakat administrators
4. Those whose hearts are to be reconciled
5. Freeing slaves
6. Those in debt
7. In the path of Allah
8. Travelers in need

### 4. Sawm (الصوم) - Fasting in Ramadan
Fasting during the month of Ramadan is the fourth pillar.

**Ramadan Fasting:**
- Abstaining from food, drink, and marital relations from dawn to sunset
- Spiritual purification and self-discipline
- Increased devotion and charity
- Night prayers (Tarawih)
- Laylat al-Qadr (Night of Power)

**Exemptions:**
- Sick people
- Travelers
- Pregnant or nursing women
- Elderly unable to fast
- Children before puberty

### 5. Hajj (الحج) - Pilgrimage to Mecca
The pilgrimage to Mecca is the fifth pillar, required once in a lifetime for those able.

**Hajj Rituals:**
- Ihram (pilgrim dress and state)
- Tawaf (circumambulation of Kaaba)
- Sa'i (walking between Safa and Marwah)
- Standing at Arafat
- Stoning of the devil at Mina
- Sacrifice and celebration of Eid al-Adha

## Six Articles of Faith (أركان الإيمان الستة)

### 1. Belief in Allah (الإيمان بالله)
- Monotheism (Tawhid)
- Allah's 99 Beautiful Names (Asma ul-Husna)
- Divine attributes of perfection

### 2. Belief in Angels (الإيمان بالملائكة)
- Created from light
- Servants of Allah
- Notable angels: Jibril, Mikail, Israfil, Azrael

### 3. Belief in Divine Books (الإيمان بالكتب)
- Quran (final revelation)
- Torah, Gospel, Psalms
- Previous scriptures were altered over time

### 4. Belief in Prophets (الإيمان بالرسل)
- 124,000 prophets sent by Allah
- 25 mentioned by name in Quran
- Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) is the final messenger

### 5. Belief in Day of Judgment (الإيمان باليوم الآخر)
- Resurrection after death
- Divine judgment
- Paradise (Jannah) and Hell (Jahannam)

### 6. Belief in Divine Decree (الإيمان بالقدر)
- Allah's knowledge of all things
- Divine predestination
- Human free will and responsibility

## Important Prophets in Islam

### Prophet Adam (آدم عليه السلام)
- First human and prophet
- Father of humanity
- Taught by Allah directly

### Prophet Ibrahim (إبراهيم عليه السلام)
- Father of Prophets
- Built the Kaaba with Ismail
- Passed the test of sacrificing his son

### Prophet Musa (موسى عليه السلام)
- Leader of Bani Israel
- Received the Torah
- Performed miracles in Egypt

### Prophet Isa (عيسى عليه السلام)
- Born of Virgin Mary
- Performed miracles by Allah's permission
- Will return before Day of Judgment

### Prophet Muhammad (محمد صلى الله عليه وسلم)
- Final messenger of Allah
- Received the Quran
- Perfect example for humanity

## Islamic Calendar and Important Dates

### Hijri Months:
1. **Muharram** - Sacred month, contains Ashura
2. **Safar** - Month of travel
3. **Rabi' al-Awwal** - Birth month of Prophet Muhammad
4. **Rabi' al-Thani** - Second spring
5. **Jumada al-Awwal** - First of parched land
6. **Jumada al-Thani** - Second of parched land
7. **Rajab** - Sacred month
8. **Sha'ban** - Month of separation
9. **Ramadan** - Month of fasting
10. **Shawwal** - Month of hunting, contains Eid al-Fitr
11. **Dhul Qi'dah** - Sacred month
12. **Dhul Hijjah** - Month of Hajj, contains Eid al-Adha

### Important Islamic Dates:
- **Laylat al-Qadr** - Night of Power (last 10 nights of Ramadan)
- **Eid al-Fitr** - Festival of Breaking the Fast
- **Eid al-Adha** - Festival of Sacrifice
- **Day of Arafat** - 9th of Dhul Hijjah
- **Ashura** - 10th of Muharram

## Women in Islam

### Rights of Women:
- Right to education and knowledge
- Right to work and earn
- Right to own property
- Right to choose spouse
- Right to inheritance
- Right to participate in society

### Honored Positions:
- Mothers given special status
- Wives as partners in faith
- Daughters as blessings
- Scholars and teachers

### Notable Women in Islamic History:
- **Khadijah (RA)** - First wife of Prophet, first Muslim
- **Aisha (RA)** - Scholar and teacher
- **Fatimah (RA)** - Daughter of Prophet
- **Maryam (AS)** - Mother of Prophet Isa

## Islamic Ethics and Morals

### Core Values:
- **Justice (العدل)** - Fairness in all dealings
- **Compassion (الرحمة)** - Mercy towards all creation
- **Honesty (الصدق)** - Truthfulness in speech and action
- **Patience (الصبر)** - Perseverance through difficulties
- **Gratitude (الشكر)** - Thankfulness to Allah

### Prohibited Actions (Haram):
- Shirk (associating partners with Allah)
- Murder
- Adultery
- Theft
- Consuming intoxicants
- Eating pork
- Usury (Riba)
- Gambling

### Recommended Actions (Mustahabb):
- Seeking knowledge
- Helping the needy
- Visiting the sick
- Being kind to parents
- Smiling at others
- Environmental care
